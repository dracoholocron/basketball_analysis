# Scenario: Post-Game Review

**Audience**: Coach and analytics staff  
**Time required**: 1-2 hours (mostly waiting for video analysis)  
**Goal**: Turn game film into player insights and discussion points

---

## Step 1: Upload Game Video

1. Go to **Games** in the sidebar
2. Click your game → scroll to "Upload Video"
3. Drag and drop the game file (MP4 recommended) or click to browse
4. Click **Upload** — an analysis job starts automatically

---

## Step 2: Monitor the Analysis Job

1. Go to **Analysis Jobs** in the sidebar
2. Find your job — it shows the current stage and % complete
3. Typical stages: `ingestion → tracking → annotation → export`
4. When status shows **Done**, the video is ready

---

## Step 3: View the Annotated Video

1. Return to **Games → [your game]**
2. Click **Download Annotated Video** to save the tracked version
3. Or click **View with Overlay** to play the video in-browser with bounding boxes

In the overlay player, every player tracked on-screen has a colored bounding box. You can toggle between:
- **Bounding Boxes** — rectangles around each player
- **Skeleton** — joint-by-joint skeleton overlay
- **Both** — combined

---

## Step 4: Review Event Heatmap

1. From the Game Tracker, click **Event Heatmap** for this matchup
2. Review the shot chart — hot zones are highlighted in red
3. Check your Keys to Victory progress bars to see which ones were achieved

---

## Step 5: Share with Players

1. Navigate to the game with the overlay active
2. Pause on specific plays to show individual players their positioning
3. Use the **Coach Notes** field in the Scouting Report to add feedback

---

## Tips
- Video analysis takes 5-20 minutes depending on file size; you can do other tasks while it runs
- The overlay works best on desktop; tablet also works reasonably well
- If the bounding box tracking looks wrong on a player, it may indicate a jersey color conflict — this is a known limitation with multi-team identical kits
